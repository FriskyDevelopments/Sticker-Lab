# Frisky Developments

## Brand role

Frisky Developments is the parent creative studio behind STIX MΛGIC and LORE.

It is not a cold software company, a generic development shop, or an abstract innovation platform. It is the signature behind the products: the warm and slightly mischievous creative force that gives the ecosystem its spark.

If STIX is the magic and LORE gives that magic identity, Frisky is the one that made the invitation feel alive in the first place.

## Positioning

**Frisky Developments = the playful creator behind the magic.**

This positioning matters because the parent brand should add authorship and character without competing with the product brands. Frisky should feel like the maker's presence: confident, human, generous, and unmistakably intentional.

## Narrative

Frisky Developments should read as the giver in the ecosystem.

It brings the emotional charge, the sense of invitation, and the feeling that the products were made by a real creative intelligence rather than assembled by a faceless company. The brand should imply craft, delight, and discernment.

The tone is premium, but never sterile. Playful, but never sloppy. Warm, but not sentimental.

## Archetype

### The Mischievous Giver

Frisky's core archetype is **The Mischievous Giver**.

That means the brand should feel:

- generous rather than extractive
- playful rather than performatively quirky
- alive rather than over-designed
- confident enough to smile
- slightly irreverent, but still tasteful

The mischief is small and controlled. It shows up as charm, timing, wit, and a sense of motion. It should never tip into chaos, gimmick, or juvenile cleverness.

## Relationship to STIX MΛGIC and LORE

### Frisky Developments and STIX MΛGIC
Frisky is the author. STIX is the active magic.

Frisky should endorse, frame, and sign the system. STIX should remain the lead mark when the product experience is in motion.

### Frisky Developments and LORE
Frisky creates the world and tone that make identity feel meaningful. LORE is the layer where that identity becomes visible to the user.

Frisky should not replace LORE's role as the user's presence layer. It sits one level above it as the studio sensibility behind the ecosystem.

### Short ecosystem statement

- **Frisky gives the magic.**
- **STIX is the magic.**
- **LORE gives that magic identity.**

## Voice and tone

### Voice attributes

Frisky Developments should sound:

- warm
- human
- generous
- playful
- design-aware
- slightly mischievous
- precise enough to feel premium

### Tone guidance

Use language that feels crafted, conversational, and intentional. Avoid flattening the brand into generic tech vocabulary.

Preferred qualities:

- clear over buzzword-heavy
- elegant over grandiose
- inviting over salesy
- expressive over corporate
- lightly witty over loudly clever

Avoid:

- startup clichés about disruption, innovation, or scale
- enterprise seriousness
- detached AI-language that feels synthetic or sterile
- excessive mythology unsupported by the actual product
- fluffy copy that says little about what the brand does

### Example tonal direction

Good Frisky language tends to feel like:

- a creator signing their work
- a studio introducing something with delight
- a system invitation with a little wink in it

It should not feel like:

- a venture-backed software homepage
- a consultancy brochure
- a faceless product platform statement

## Logo direction

### What the parent logo should communicate

The Frisky Developments mark should communicate:

- authorship
- warmth
- invitation
- motion
- a controlled spark of mischief
- the sense of a creative signature rather than a corporate stamp

A good parent mark should feel like the presence behind the ecosystem, not the loudest object in it.

### Formal characteristics to explore

The logo system should favor:

- soft curves over hard-edged geometry
- slightly irregular or living forms over mechanically perfect ones
- restrained expressiveness
- a feeling of offering, gesture, spark, or release
- subtle character presence, even if the mark remains abstract

The ideal result is a mark that feels alive without becoming cartoonish.

### What to avoid

Avoid:

- sharp corporate geometry
- sterile SaaS minimalism
- blue-purple startup cliché gradients
- severe enterprise symmetry
- visuals that compete with STIX MΛGIC as the hero mark
- over-illustrated mascots or novelty marks that cheapen the system

### How it should differ from STIX MΛGIC

Frisky should feel more like a signature; STIX should feel more like the magic engine.

In practical terms:

- **Frisky** can be softer, more personal, and more gestural.
- **STIX** can hold more intensity, precision, and visual charge.
- **Frisky** should frame the ecosystem.
- **STIX** should dominate moments of product performance and transformation.

If both appear together, Frisky should act as the quiet source and STIX as the active expression.

### How it should differ from LORE

LORE should feel closer to identity, profile, and user presence. Frisky should feel like the maker's signature above that layer.

If LORE becomes intimate or portrait-like, Frisky should remain more authorial and emblematic.

## Practical implementation guidance

### Parent-brand placements

Frisky Developments is best used in:

- footers and end cards
- about sections
- ecosystem diagrams
- brand books and design system references
- launch notes or editorial surfaces where authorship matters

It should be used more sparingly inside core STIX product interactions.

### Copy pattern guidance

Useful parent-brand phrasing includes:

- "By Frisky Developments"
- "A Frisky Developments project"
- "Built by Frisky Developments"
- "From Frisky Developments"

These frames preserve the studio's presence without confusing the product hierarchy.

## Repo audit summary

Within this repository, STIX already has a clear systems narrative, but Frisky Developments is only implied and LORE is not yet defined in brand terms. That leaves the ecosystem legible to insiders but incomplete for future collaborators.

This document establishes Frisky as the parent creative studio, gives it a usable tonal and visual brief, and clarifies how it should sit above — not on top of — STIX MΛGIC and LORE.
